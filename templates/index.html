AVERAGE PERCENTAGE OF PUBLIC(GOVERNMENT) TELEVISION STATIONS

There exists certain countries that are governed by a strict military regime which prevents private companies from owning or operating broadcast media to prevent/curb spread of slander against the government. While some other countries simply have too low a population which is not enough motivation to allow private investors to establish broadcasting businesses there. Therefore both categories of countries have 100% of their television stations owned by the government. Such Nations include Algeria, Carpe Verde, Comoros, Eritea, Eswantini, Gambia amongst others.

ZIMBABWE(A CASE STUDY) - The Government owns all local radio and TV stations; foreign shortwave broadcasts and satellite TV are available to those who can afford antennas and receivers; in rural areas, access to TV broadcasts is extremely limited; analog TV only, no digital service (2017)
